🗣️ Linguistic Reasoning (Lisān al-ʿArab Analysis)

Subḥān (سُبْحَانَ): Root: س ب ح — to swim, to move freely, to glorify. Signifies removing Allah from any imperfection.

Biḥamdih (بِحَمْدِهِ): Root: ح م د — praise mixed with love and reverence.

ʿAdada khalqihi (عَدَدَ خَلْقِهِ): Emphasizes praise as vast as the number of His creations.

Riḍā nafsihi (رِضَا نَفْسِهِ): As much as Allah is pleased with.

Zīnata ʿarshihi (زِنَةَ عَرْشِهِ): As weighty as the Throne — metaphor of utmost magnitude.

Midāda kalimātihi (مِدَادَ كَلِمَاتِهِ): As expansive as the ink needed to write His words — referring to limitless expressions of divine will (cf. Qur'an 18:109).

Each clause compounds the magnitude of glorification, making this dhikr exceptionally rewarding.
